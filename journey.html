St. Patricks School

My secondary school life was my most memorable time in my education journey, as I was exposed to a wider range of activities in school, as well as a brand new culture of brotherhood. I joined the St Patrick's School Military Band (SPSMB) .

In the band, we learned how to play the instruments from scratch, as well as basic UG (Uniform Group) Drills. I really enjoyed my time there and forged strong bonds with my bandmates, whom I'm still close to today.

Through the lessons in school, the countless performances I practised for, the different responsibilities taken up during CCA and in school, it really provided me with a strong foundation of who I am today.

Victoria Junior College

My Junior College life was a whole new experience, as none of my friends from my secondary school came over. Thankfully, there were many like-minded individuals in my class and we soon became closer to each other after the countless sessions of late study sessions we put ourselves through.

Project Work was one of the most memorable periods of JC, where we had to stay up late many nights collaborating and designing the product and solution for our project. Days of presentations and practises led to a very fruitful experience as a whole as we all put our best effort and time into getting it right.

I joined the MindSports club in my JC where we focused on competitions related to International Chess, Chinese Chess and Professional Contract Bridge. Soon, I took over the role of Chairperson and was in charge of running the CCA as a whole, planning the different events, training sessions and liaising with coaches on training arrangments. Overall, my JC experience was holistic and fruitful. I attribute this to my teacher who told us on the first day - Grades are not everything in JC, but the memories you make are.
